Russian Blini

The origins of blini can be traced back to ancient Slavic and Russian traditions.
Blini were initially prepared as a way to celebrate the arrival of spring and the end of winter.
The round shape of blini symbolized the sun, and their consumption was believed to bring good luck and ward off evil spirits.

History

In ancient times, blini were an important part of pagan rituals and festivals, especially during Maslenitsa, a pre-Christian holiday celebrating the end of winter and the coming of spring.
Maslenitsa is often referred to as "Butter Week" in English, and during this time, people would eat blini made with butter and other rich ingredients to ensure a good harvest and prosperity.
