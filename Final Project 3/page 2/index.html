Isaac Levitan did not receive a formal art education, yet his paintings became part of Tretyakov's collection, and were showcased at the World Exhibition in Paris.


At the age of 38, Levitan was awarded the title of Academician in landscape painting and began teaching  at the School of Painting, Sculpture, and Architecture.


Ilya Repin developed a passion for drawing in early childhood. He initially trained as a topographer and apprenticed with icon painters. Repin gained admission to the Academy of Arts on his second attempt and later returned to teach there.


Repin received commissions for paintings from renowned St. Petersburg aristocrats, and even  Emperor Alexander III commissioned works from him.


"King of the Forest" – that's what Ivan Shishkin's contemporaries called him. He traveled extensively throughout Russia, extolling the majestic beauty of its nature in his paintings, which are now known to everyone.


Classmates and even teachers noted that "Shishkin paints views that no one before him has painted: just a field, forest, river – and they come out as beautifully as Swiss landscapes."
